## 1. Purpose

This plan defines who receives what information, when, through which channel, and in what format throughout the project. Its objective is to ensure that all stakeholders have the information they need to make decisions and stay aligned.

## 4. Channel Guidelines

### Microsoft Teams

- **General** project channel: formal announcements, reports, documented decisions
- Direct chat: quick coordination between two people
- Specific channels (Development, QA, etc.): technical discussions by area
- Expected response time: within 4 hours during business hours

### Email

- For formal communications with external stakeholders or from other companies
- Always include in subject: `[PROJECT] — [Topic]`
- Expected response time: 24 business hours
- Do not use for urgent decisions

### WhatsApp

- Only for urgent notifications requiring immediate response
- Do not document decisions here — move any agreement to Teams or email
- Do not use to share project documents or files

### Microsoft Planner

- Source of truth for task status
- All meeting agreements that involve work must end up as a task in Planner

---

## 5. Expectations Management — Principles

1. **Proactivity over reactivity:** The PM communicates problems before the sponsor asks.
2. **No surprises:** If there is a risk that could affect delivery, communicate at least 5 days in advance.
3. **Written record:** Every verbally agreed decision is confirmed in writing via Teams or email.
4. **Appropriate frequency:** Not too frequent (creates noise) nor too sporadic (creates uncertainty).
5. **Format by audience:** For management: executive summary. For technical team: operational detail.
