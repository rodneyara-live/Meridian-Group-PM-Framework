# SOP-008 — Communication and Stakeholder Management

**Effective communication management with stakeholders in Meridian Group portfolio projects**

---

## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

Manage effective communication with all project stakeholders, ensuring that the right information reaches the right people at the right time through the appropriate channel.

### 1.2 Scope

Applies to all strategic portfolio projects from the Initiation phase through Closure.

### 1.3 References

- FOR-011: Communication Plan
- SOP-002: Project Initiation Process
- SOP-004: Project Control and Monitoring

---

## 2. Stakeholder Identification and Classification

### 2.1 Stakeholder Matrix

For each project, a matrix must be completed with:

| Stakeholder | Role in the Project | Influence Level | Interest Level | Strategy |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| [Name] | [Role] | High / Medium / Low | High / Medium / Low | Manage closely / Keep satisfied / Keep informed / Monitor |

### 2.2 Strategies by Quadrant

- **High influence + High interest** → Manage closely. Frequent, bidirectional communication.
- **High influence + Low interest** → Keep satisfied. Periodic updates without overload.
- **Low influence + High interest** → Keep informed. Regular reports, few decisions.
- **Low influence + Low interest** → Monitor. Minimum communication, only when relevant.

## 3. Communication Plan (FOR-011)

For each project, a Communication Plan is documented with:

| Communication | Audience | Frequency | Channel | Responsible | Format |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

## 4. Communication Channels

### 4.1 Microsoft Teams

**Usage**: Team meetings, quick communication, document sharing, project channels.

**Norms**:
- Thematic channels per project to maintain context
- Messages in the corresponding channel (not DMs if the topic is team-related)
- Expected response within the business day

### 4.2 Email

**Usage**: Formal reports, communications with external stakeholders, documents for signature.

**Norms**:
- Clear subject line with project name in brackets: `[PROJECT] Subject`
- Expected response within 24 hours on business days
- Use CC judiciously, only to those who truly need the information

### 4.3 WhatsApp

**Usage**: Urgencies, quick coordination, critical blocker alerts.

**Norms**:
- Only for urgent or low-formality communications
- Not a channel for formal approvals
- Decisions made via WhatsApp must be confirmed in Teams or email

### 4.4 Microsoft Planner

**Usage**: Task status, work assignments, comments on specific tasks.

**Norms**:
- Every relevant comment on a task is written in the task (not in Teams/WhatsApp)
- Task status is updated by the responsible person, not by the PM

## 5. Communication by Situation Type

### 5.1 Routine Communication

- Weekly reports (FOR-007) sent every Friday
- Kanban board update before each bi-weekly meeting
- Summary of milestones achieved when closing each phase

### 5.2 Change Communication

- Approved changes communicated at the next bi-weekly meeting
- Critical changes communicated immediately to the affected team
- Every scope change communicated to the Strategic Committee

### 5.3 Risk and Issue Communication

- Critical issues: immediate notification via WhatsApp + Teams to PM and Sponsor
- New risks: included in the weekly report
- Resolved issues: communicated to the team with the solution implemented

### 5.4 Milestone and Achievement Communication

- Explicitly celebrate milestones achieved with the team
- Inform the Strategic Committee of significant progress
- Document phase closures in the weekly report

---

## 6. Expectations Management

### 6.1 Principles

- Communicate bad news early, with context and solution options
- Do not promise dates without consulting the technical team
- Be specific: avoid ambiguities in status ("almost ready" → "85% completed, available Friday")
- Confirm understanding: verify that the stakeholder understood the key communication

### 6.2 Sponsor Alignment Meetings

When a project enters 🟡 or 🔴 status, the PM convenes an alignment meeting with the sponsor within 24h, with:
- Current status and root cause
- Resolution options with impacts
- Required decision and deadline

---

## 7. Roles and Responsibilities

### 7.1 Project Manager

- Develop and maintain the Communication Plan (FOR-011)
- Execute routine communication (reports, meetings)
- Manage communications with all stakeholder levels
- Escalate and communicate issues and risks within defined timelines

### 7.2 Project Sponsor

- Communicate any changes in expectations or priorities to the PM
- Be the communication channel with external high-level stakeholders
- Respond to PM reports and communications within 24h

### 7.3 Project Team

- Communicate blockers, risks, and progress to the PM in real time
- Participate in team meetings
- Keep task status updated in Planner
