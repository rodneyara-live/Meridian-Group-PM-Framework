# SOP-005 — Change Management in Projects

**Control of modifications to scope, schedule, or resources of active projects**

---

## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

Control and manage in an orderly manner any modification to the scope, schedule, or resources of a project, ensuring that changes are evaluated, approved, and communicated before implementation.

### 1.2 Scope

Applies to all active projects in the Meridian Group and Meridian Pay portfolio. Every change that affects scope, deliverables, schedule, or resources must follow this process.

### 1.3 References

- FOR-009: Change Request
- SOP-003: Requirements Gathering and Management
- SOP-004: Project Control and Monitoring

---

## 2. Types of Change

| Type | Description | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Scope** | Addition, removal, or modification of requirements | Adding a new communication channel to the virtual agent |
| **Schedule** | Modification of dates or milestones | Moving the go-live date by 2 weeks |
| **Resources** | Change in assigned people or dedication time | Reducing a contractor's availability |
| **Budget** | Variation in estimated costs | Additional implementation hours |

---

## 3. Classification by Impact

### Minor Change (<20% of remaining effort)

- Adjustment of acceptance criteria without changing the objective
- Refinement of already defined stories
- Correction of documentation errors

**Process**: Email to sponsor + update in Planner. No formal form required.

### Major Change (≥20% of remaining effort or affects milestones)

- New unplanned user stories
- Technology or platform change
- Modification of committed delivery dates

**Process**: Full FOR-009 + approval according to authority level.

---

## 4. Change Management Process

### 4.1 Step 1 — Identification and Request

**Who can request it**: Any team member, sponsor, or stakeholder.

**Activities**:
1. Identify and describe the proposed change
2. Complete FOR-009 (Change Request) for major changes
3. Send to Project Manager for evaluation

### 4.2 Step 2 — Impact Evaluation

**Responsible**: Project Manager + technical team (if applicable).

**Analyze**:
- Impact on schedule (additional days)
- Impact on effort (additional hours)
- Impact on other projects or dependencies
- Risks introduced by the change
- Estimated additional cost

**Deadline**: Maximum 2 business days for major changes.

### 4.3 Step 3 — Approval

| Type of Change | Approver |
| --- | --- |
| Minor (without affecting milestones) | Project Sponsor |
| Major (affects schedule <2 weeks) | Sponsor + PM |
| Critical (affects milestones, resources, or significant scope) | Strategic Committee |

### 4.4 Step 4 — Implementation

Once approved:
1. Update the Requirements Matrix (FOR-003)
2. Adjust the schedule in Planner
3. Notify the team at the next bi-weekly meeting
4. Record the change in the Change Request log (FOR-009)

### 4.5 Step 5 — Communication

- PM communicates the approved change to the team at the bi-weekly meeting
- Sponsor notifies external stakeholders if applicable
- It is documented in the Weekly Status Report (FOR-007)

---

## 5. Criteria for Rejecting a Change

A change may be rejected when:
- It contradicts the project's strategic objectives
- The impact on schedule or budget is unacceptable
- It generates unmanageable critical dependencies
- The Strategic Committee determines it is not aligned with portfolio priorities

A rejected change is documented in FOR-009 with the justification.

---

## 6. Emergency Changes

For urgent changes that cannot wait for the normal process:
1. Verbal authorization from sponsor or Committee with retroactive documentation within 24h
2. PM records the change in Planner immediately
3. FOR-009 completed within the following 24h
4. Immediate communication to the affected team

---

## 7. Roles and Responsibilities

### 7.1 Project Manager

- Evaluate the impact of all requested changes
- Facilitate the approval process
- Update documentation and Planner after approval
- Communicate changes to the team and stakeholders

### 7.2 Project Sponsor

- Approve or reject changes within their authority level
- Provide business context to evaluate impact
- Escalate to the Committee when the change requires it

### 7.3 Strategic Committee

- Approve high-impact changes
- Resolve priority conflicts between projects
- Communicate decisions to the portfolio

### 7.4 Change Requester

- Clearly document the change and its justification
- Participate in impact evaluation when required
- Respect the decision of the corresponding approval level
